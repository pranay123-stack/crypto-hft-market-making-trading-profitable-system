================================================================================
                          KEY FEATURES
================================================================================

Each Checklist Includes:
- Step-by-step procedures with clear actions
- Expected values and acceptable ranges
- Pass/fail criteria for each step
- What to do if checks fail
- Escalation procedures with severity levels
- Sign-off requirements
- Time estimates for completion
- Command examples where applicable
- Troubleshooting guidance

Escalation Levels:
- SEVERITY LEVEL 1: Critical (0-5 min response) - Trading halted
- SEVERITY LEVEL 2: High (5-15 min response) - Degraded performance
- SEVERITY LEVEL 3: Medium (15-60 min response) - Minor issues
- SEVERITY LEVEL 4: Low (same day response) - Routine items

Sign-Off Requirements:
- Primary operator signature
- Secondary reviewer signature (for production)
- Timestamp (UTC)
- Overall status (PASS/FAIL/CONDITIONAL PASS)

Documentation Requirements:
- All checklists must be saved to: /var/log/hft/checklists/YYYY/MM/DD/
- 7-year retention for regulatory compliance
- Backup to compliance archive
- Available for audit review

================================================================================
                          CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS
================================================================================

1. Start checklists early (minimum 2 hours before market open)
2. Never skip steps - document exceptions instead
3. Communicate failures immediately
4. Follow escalation procedures
5. Document everything thoroughly
6. All trading requires completed checklists
7. Conditional passes require Risk Manager approval

PROHIBITED ACTIONS:
- Trading without completed checklists
- Deploying code during market hours (without emergency approval)
- Overriding risk limits without approval
- Ignoring failed checklist items
- Skipping sign-off procedures
