STRATEGIC ALTERNATIVES
----------------------

OPTION 1: Remain Independent and Profitable

Scenario: Continue growing profitably without outside capital after Series A

Advantages:
- Maintain control and independence
- Build long-term value
- Dividend potential for investors
- Flexibility in strategy
- No pressure from new investors

Challenges:
- Slower growth (no Series B fuel)
- Competitive pressure
- Founder/employee liquidity limited
- Investors lack liquidity event

Financial projections (independent path):
- Year 3: $50M revenue, $10M EBITDA
- Year 5: $120M revenue, $45M EBITDA
- Year 7: $200M revenue, $85M EBITDA

Investor return options:
- Dividend distributions (after profitability)
- Secondary sales (find buyer for shares)
- Eventual sale in Year 7-10

OPTION 2: Strategic Partnership + Partial Exit

Scenario: Partner with exchange or trading firm, sell 20-40% stake

Example: Binance acquires 30% for $200M (implies $667M valuation)

Advantages:
- Partial liquidity for founders/investors
- Strategic benefits (access to Binance customers)
- Remain independent
- Validation of value

Investor return:
Series A investors own 20.4% fully-diluted
30% sale at $667M valuation = 20.4% worth $136M
If 30% of shares sold proportionally: $40.8M proceeds (3.4x)
Still own 14.3% of company worth $95M
Total value: $136M (11.3x)

OPTION 3: Acqui-hire

Scenario: Acquire company primarily for talent, technology is secondary

Typical valuation: $1M-$3M per key person

For us: 35 people (Year 2) × $2M = $70M valuation
Too low for investors - not desirable outcome

Only makes sense if:
- Company failing / running out of cash
- No other buyers
- Need liquidity urgently

Avoid this outcome through: Strong execution, maintaining runway

OPTION 4: Merger with Competitor

Scenario: Merge with another HFT infrastructure provider

Candidates:
- Trading Technologies (if goes public again)
- FlexTrade
- Larger crypto-native platform

Structure: Stock + cash
Example: 50% stock in combined entity, 50% cash

Advantages:
- Combined scale and resources
- Eliminate competitor
- Synergies in product and go-to-market

Investor return: Depends on combined entity value and structure
Typically 5-15x return range

EXIT TIMING ANALYSIS
--------------------

OPTIMAL EXIT TIMING:

Too early (Years 1-3):
- Valuation too low (pre-product/market fit)
- Haven't captured market opportunity
- Multiple likely 3-6x revenue (low)
- Investor return: 5-15x (acceptable but not great)

Sweet spot (Years 4-6):
- Proven business model
- Strong growth trajectory
- Market leadership established
- Multiple: 8-15x revenue
- Investor return: 15-40x (excellent)

Too late (Years 7-10):
- Growth slowing
- Market saturated
- Multiple compression (6-10x revenue)
- May be most profitable but lower valuations
- Better for long-term hold, not maximizing investor return

Our recommendation: Target Year 5-6 exit
- 2029-2030 timeframe
- Revenue $150M-$250M
- Clear market leadership
- Strong strategic value

MARKET CONDITIONS IMPACT
------------------------

Bull market (crypto prices up, lots of trading):
- High trading volumes = more revenue
- Strategic buyers aggressive
- High valuation multiples (12-20x revenue)
- Best time to sell

Bear market (crypto down, low trading):
- Lower trading volumes = less revenue
- Buyers cautious
- Lower multiples (6-10x revenue)
- Wait if possible

Regulatory environment impact:
- Positive regulation (MiCA, US clarity): Higher valuations
- Negative regulation (bans, restrictions): Lower valuations
- Uncertainty: Discount to valuation (10-30%)

Our strategy:
- Build business that works in all environments
- Time exit for favorable conditions if possible
- Have backup plans for various scenarios

NEGOTIATION STRATEGY
--------------------

Maximizing exit value:

1. Create competition among buyers
   - Approach multiple categories simultaneously
   - Never exclusive until final stages
   - Auction process if possible

2. Establish BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)
   - IPO as alternative
   - Continue independently
   - Strengthens negotiating position

3. Highlight strategic value beyond financials
   - Team expertise
   - Technology differentiation
   - Customer relationships
   - Market position

4. Timing leverage
   - Sell from strength, not desperation
   - Have 18-24 months runway always
   - Walk away power

5. Structure optimization
   - Cash vs stock mix
   - Earnouts (if confident in growth)
   - Employment agreements for founders
   - Retention packages for team

TAX CONSIDERATIONS
------------------

For investors (US tax law):

Holding period: >1 year for long-term capital gains
Tax rate: 20% federal (long-term capital gains)
Plus: 3.8% net investment income tax
Total: 23.8% effective tax rate

Example (base case $133M proceeds on $12M investment):
Gain: $121M
Tax: $28.8M (23.8%)
After-tax proceeds: $104.2M
After-tax return: 8.7x

For founders (Qualified Small Business Stock):

If qualify for QSBS (IRC Section 1202):
- 100% exclusion (if held >5 years)
- $10M or 10x basis (whichever greater) exempt
- Significant tax savings

Example for Sarah (own $95M):
Without QSBS: $22.6M tax (23.8%)
With QSBS: $0 tax (if qualify)
Savings: $22.6M

Requirements:
- C-corporation
- <$50M in assets when issued
- Held >5 years
- Active business (not passive investment)

Our structure: Should qualify for QSBS
Major benefit for founders and early employees
