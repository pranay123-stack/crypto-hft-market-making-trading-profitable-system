                    1. BUDGET PLANNING OVERVIEW
================================================================================

PURPOSE OF BUDGET PLANNING:
---------------------------

Budget planning serves critical functions:
    1. Financial Control: Prevent cost overruns
    2. Resource Allocation: Prioritize investments
    3. Performance Tracking: Measure actual vs plan
    4. Strategic Planning: Align costs with business goals
    5. Accountability: Hold teams responsible for their budgets

BUDGET PLANNING CYCLE:
----------------------

Annual Budget (November - December):
    - Set annual cost targets
    - Allocate budget by category and team
    - Get executive approval
    - Finalize by December 15 for January 1 start

Quarterly Reviews (End of Q1, Q2, Q3):
    - Analyze variance vs budget
    - Adjust forecasts for remainder of year
    - Realloc ate unused budget
    - Identify cost risks and opportunities

Monthly Tracking (1st week of each month):
    - Compare actual vs budget
    - Investigate variances >10%
    - Update rolling 3-month forecast
    - Report to finance and executives

BUDGET CATEGORIES:
------------------

We budget across 5 major categories:

1. Infrastructure ($8.1M annually)
   - Colocation facilities
   - Network connectivity
   - Hardware depreciation

2. Trading Costs ($11.3M annually)
   - Exchange fees (net of rebates)
   - Market impact & slippage
   - Clearing & settlement

3. Market Data ($2.0M annually)
   - Real-time data feeds
   - Historical data
   - Alternative data

4. Cloud Compute ($1.2M annually)
   - Compute instances
   - Storage
   - Data transfer

5. Research & Development ($2.5M annually)*
   *Not included in daily operational costs, separate R&D budget

Total Operational Budget: $22.6M annually

BUDGET ALLOCATION PRINCIPLES:
-----------------------------

1. BASE ON ACTUALS
   Use previous year actual costs as baseline
   Adjust for known changes (contract renewals, new strategies)

2. ADD BUFFER
   Include 5-10% contingency for unexpected costs
   Don't budget at 100% of capacity (leaves no room for variance)

3. PRIORITIZE BY ROI
   Allocate more to high-ROI cost categories
   Cut or constrain low-ROI spending

4. ALIGN WITH STRATEGY
   Budget supports business goals
   If goal is expansion, budget for growth
   If goal is efficiency, budget cost reductions

5. BE REALISTIC
   Aggressive targets are demotivating
   Sandbagging wastes resources
   Find the right balance

BUDGET RESPONSIBILITY:
----------------------

Cost Category        Owner                  Approval Authority
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Infrastructure       VP Engineering         CTO
Trading Costs        Head of Trading        CEO
Market Data          Head of Quant Research CTO
Cloud Compute        Head of Data Science   CTO
Total Budget         CFO                    CEO + Board

Each budget owner is accountable for staying within their allocation.

VARIANCE CATEGORIES:
--------------------

We classify variances as:

Favorable Variance (FV): Actual < Budget (spending less)
Unfavorable Variance (UV): Actual > Budget (spending more)

Variance Thresholds:
    Minor: 0-5% variance
    Moderate: 5-15% variance
    Major: >15% variance

Response by Threshold:
    Minor: Monitor, no action required
    Moderate: Investigate root cause, may need mitigation
    Major: Urgent review, requires exec approval to continue
