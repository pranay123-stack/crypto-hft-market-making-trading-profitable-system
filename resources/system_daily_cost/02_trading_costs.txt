UNDERSTANDING MAKER-TAKER PRICING:
-----------------------------------

Maker Orders: Provide liquidity to the market
    - Limit orders that rest on the order book
    - Earn rebates (typically $0.0015-$0.0030/share)
    - Lower priority than immediate executions

Taker Orders: Remove liquidity from the market
    - Market orders or marketable limit orders
    - Pay fees (typically $0.0025-$0.0030/share)
    - Immediate execution

UNDERSTANDING MARKET IMPACT:
----------------------------

Market Impact = Price movement caused by our trading activity

Two Components:
1. Temporary Impact: Price moves while we trade, then reverts
2. Permanent Impact: Long-lasting price change from information signal

For HFT market making, we primarily experience temporary impact.

MARKET IMPACT MODELS:
---------------------

Square-Root Model (Industry Standard):
    Impact = α × σ × sqrt(Q / V) × Sign

    Where:
        α = Empirical constant (typically 0.3-0.5)
        σ = Daily volatility
        Q = Order size (shares)
        V = Average daily volume
        Sign = +1 for buy, -1 for sell

Example Calculation:
    Stock: SPY (S&P 500 ETF)
    Daily Volume: 80,000,000 shares
    Volatility: 15% annualized = 0.95% daily
    Our Order: 10,000 shares (buy)
    Current Price: $450

    Impact = 0.4 × 0.0095 × sqrt(10,000 / 80,000,000) × 450
           = 0.4 × 0.0095 × 0.01118 × 450
           = $0.0191 per share

    Total Cost: 10,000 × $0.0191 = $191

Linear Model (for large orders):
    Impact = β × (Q / V) × Price

    Where β is typically 0.1-0.3

    Same Example:
    Impact = 0.2 × (10,000 / 80,000,000) × 450
           = 0.2 × 0.000125 × 450
           = $0.01125 per share

    Total Cost: 10,000 × $0.01125 = $112.50

We use the square-root model as it's more accurate for our order sizes.

UNDERSTANDING SPREAD COSTS:
---------------------------

Spread = Ask Price - Bid Price

When we take liquidity (market orders), we pay the spread:
    - Buying: Pay the Ask (higher price)
    - Selling: Receive the Bid (lower price)

Half-Spread Cost = (Ask - Bid) / 2

For market making, we CAPTURE the spread. For directional trading, we PAY it.

                    6. SLIPPAGE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

DEFINING SLIPPAGE:
------------------

Slippage = Actual Execution Price - Expected Price

Sources of Slippage:
1. Market movement between order decision and execution
2. Partial fills at multiple price levels
3. Queue priority (orders ahead of us get filled first)
4. Information leakage (market anticipates our orders)

SLIPPAGE COMPONENTS:
--------------------

1. Latency Slippage:
   Time Delay: Order decision to exchange = 250 microseconds
   Price Movement: Varies by volatility

   Example:
       SPY volatility: 15% annual = 0.95% daily = 0.025% per minute
       Our latency: 250 μs = 0.0042 minutes
       Expected movement: $450 × 0.025% × 0.0042 = $0.00047 per share

       But price can move in our favor too, so net effect near zero
       for symmetric strategies.

2. Queue Position Slippage:
   When we place a limit order, we queue behind existing orders
   If price moves away before our turn, we don't fill (missed opportunity)
   If price moves through our level, we fill but at worse price than could have

   Estimated Cost: $0.0003/share on maker orders

3. Partial Fill Slippage:
   Large order fills at multiple price levels
   Average fill price worse than best price

   Example:
       Want to buy 5,000 shares of XYZ
       Offer: 1,000 @ $50.00, 1,500 @ $50.01, 2,500 @ $50.02
       Average fill: (1,000×$50.00 + 1,500×$50.01 + 2,500×$50.02) / 5,000
                   = $50.014
       Midpoint was $50.00, paid $50.014
       Slippage: $0.014/share × 5,000 = $70

4. Information Leakage Slippage:
   Our orders reveal our intentions
   Others front-run or adjust quotes
   Primarily affects large, repeated orders

   Estimated Cost: $0.0002/share on large orders
