COST OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES
-----------------------------

1. REMOTE HIRING
   - Hire remote talent for non-latency-critical roles
   - Savings: 20-30% on salaries
   - Example: Remote junior engineers at $90,000 vs $120,000

2. EQUITY COMPENSATION
   - Offer equity/profit sharing
   - Reduce cash compensation by 10-15%
   - Long-term retention benefit

3. PHASED HIRING
   - Start with smaller team (6-7)
   - Add headcount as revenue grows
   - Reduce Year 1 costs by $500,000 - $800,000

4. OPEN SOURCE CONTRIBUTIONS
   - Use existing libraries and tools
   - Contribute back to community
   - Reduce development time by 15-20%

5. AI-ASSISTED DEVELOPMENT
   - Use Claude/GPT for code generation
   - Productivity gain: 20-30%
   - Smaller team can accomplish more

6. OFFSHORE DEVELOPMENT (CAREFULLY)
   - Use for non-core components only
   - Testing, documentation, utilities
   - Cost: $40-60/hour vs $150-200/hour
   - Savings: $200,000 - $400,000/year

RECRUITMENT STRATEGIES
----------------------

1. DIRECT HIRING (Most Common)
   - Cost: 15-25% of annual salary
   - Time to hire: 2-4 months
   - Best for senior roles

2. RECRUITMENT AGENCIES
   - Cost: 20-30% of annual salary
   - Time to hire: 1-3 months
   - Useful for specialized roles

3. EMPLOYEE REFERRALS
   - Cost: $5,000 - $15,000 bonus
   - Time to hire: 1-2 months
   - Best quality hires

4. UNIVERSITY RECRUITING
   - Cost: $10,000 - $20,000 per hire
   - Fresh talent, moldable
   - Good for junior roles

5. ACQUI-HIRING
   - Acquire small teams from competitors
   - Cost: 2-3x annual salaries
   - Fast team building

RETENTION BEST PRACTICES
-------------------------

1. COMPETITIVE COMPENSATION
   - Annual market rate reviews
   - Performance bonuses (20-40% of base)
   - Profit sharing

2. CAREER DEVELOPMENT
   - Clear advancement paths
   - Mentorship programs
   - Conference attendance
   - Learning budgets

3. WORK-LIFE BALANCE
   - Flexible schedules
   - Remote work options
   - Unlimited PTO policies
   - Mental health support

4. TECHNICAL EXCELLENCE
   - Latest technology stack
   - Innovation time (20% projects)
   - Technical challenges
   - Conference speaking opportunities

5. EQUITY & OWNERSHIP
   - Stock options or profit sharing
   - Vesting schedules (4 years, 1 year cliff)
   - Meaningful ownership stakes

ATTRITION COST ANALYSIS
------------------------

Average Attrition Rate: 15-20% annually

Cost per Attrition:
- Recruitment: $30,000 - $60,000
- Lost productivity: 3-6 months of salary
- Training new hire: $20,000 - $40,000
- Knowledge loss: Incalculable

Example: Losing 2 engineers per year
- Cost: $300,000 - $600,000 per year
- Mitigation: Strong retention programs
