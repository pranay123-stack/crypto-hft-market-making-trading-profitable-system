================================================================================
OPERATIONAL PROCEDURES
================================================================================

Daily Workflow: 8:00am - Trading systems check, Pre-market risk review, 
Exchange connectivity verification. 9:00am - Trading begins, Real-time 
monitoring, Position and P&L tracking. 12:00pm - Mid-day risk review, P&L 
checkpoint. 4:00pm - Market close (for some exchanges), End-of-day 
reconciliation. 6:00pm - Daily P&L report, Risk report to management, System 
backups. Weekly: Monday: Strategy review meeting, Wednesday: Technology review, 
Friday: Week-in-review, risk committee. Monthly: Full financial close and 
reporting, Compliance review, Infrastructure maintenance window, Team all-hands 
meeting. Quarterly: Board meeting, Strategic planning, Performance reviews, 
Risk policy review. Incident Response: Severity levels (P0: trading down, P1: 
degraded, P2: minor, P3: cosmetic). Escalation procedures, Post-incident review 
(blameless postmortem), Documentation and lessons learned. Disaster Recovery: 
Offsite backups (daily), Documented DR procedures, Annual DR drill, Recovery 
time objective: <4 hours. Business Continuity: Work-from-home capability (if 
office inaccessible), Backup internet providers, Cloud-based trading (can 
operate from anywhere), Emergency contact list. Change Management: Code review 
for all changes, Staging environment testing, Deployment windows (not during 
high volatility), Rollback procedures. Documentation: Trading strategies 
documented, System architecture documented, Operational runbooks, Onboarding 
guides for new hires.
