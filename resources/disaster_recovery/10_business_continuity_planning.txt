2.2 IMPACT SCENARIOS
---------------------

Scenario 1: Complete Primary DC Loss
Impact: Unable to trade, $10M+ per day
Duration: Hours to days
Mitigation: Automatic DR site activation

Scenario 2: Cyber Attack / Ransomware
Impact: Systems encrypted, data inaccessible
Duration: Days to weeks
Mitigation: Air-gapped backups, incident response plan

Scenario 3: Key Personnel Loss
Impact: Operational disruption, knowledge loss
Duration: Weeks to months
Mitigation: Cross-training, documentation, succession planning

Scenario 4: Exchange Connectivity Loss
Impact: Cannot trade on affected exchange
Duration: Minutes to hours
Mitigation: Multi-exchange connectivity, alternative venues

Scenario 5: Pandemic / Office Inaccessible
Impact: Staff cannot access office
Duration: Weeks to months
Mitigation: Work-from-home capabilities, remote access

3. CONTINUITY STRATEGIES
================================================================================

3.1 TECHNOLOGY CONTINUITY
--------------------------

Strategy 1: Geographic Redundancy
- Primary DC: NYC (production)
- DR DC: Chicago (hot standby)
- Backup DC: London (cold storage)
- Cloud: AWS (backup, burst capacity)

Strategy 2: System Redundancy
- All critical systems N+1 redundant
- Load balanced configurations
- Automatic failover capability
- Regular failover testing

Strategy 3: Data Protection
- Synchronous replication for critical data
- Continuous backups
- Multiple backup locations
- Immutable audit trails

3.2 PEOPLE CONTINUITY
----------------------

Strategy 1: Work From Home
- All staff equipped with VPN access
- Multi-factor authentication
- Secure remote desktop
- Virtual meeting capabilities
- Home office equipment stipend

Strategy 2: Alternative Work Locations
- Backup office space in different location
- Co-working space agreements
- Hotel conference room arrangements
- Mobile trading capabilities

Strategy 3: Succession Planning
- Documented for all key roles
- Cross-training program
- Knowledge sharing sessions
- Runbook documentation
- On-call rotation

3.3 OPERATIONAL CONTINUITY
---------------------------

Strategy 1: Automated Operations
- Automated trading strategies
- Automated risk checks
- Automated reporting
- Automated reconciliation
- Minimal manual intervention required

Strategy 2: Simplified Operations Mode
- Reduce to core functions only
- Disable non-essential features
- Manual override capability
- Increased monitoring

Strategy 3: Alternative Vendors
- Backup data providers
- Alternative execution venues
- Secondary connectivity providers
- Multiple prime brokers

4.2 CRISIS RESPONSE PHASES
----------------------------

Phase 1: Detection & Alert (0-15 minutes)
- Crisis detected/reported
- Initial assessment
- CMT notification
- War room activation
- Stakeholder alert

Phase 2: Assessment & Decision (15-60 minutes)
- Situation assessment
- Impact analysis
- Options evaluation
- Decision on response
- Resource mobilization

Phase 3: Response & Mitigation (1-24 hours)
- Execute DR plans
- Activate alternatives
- Implement workarounds
- Stabilize operations
- Continuous assessment

Phase 4: Recovery (24 hours - days)
- Restore normal operations
- Validate systems
- Resume full services
- Monitor stability
- Document lessons

Phase 5: Post-Crisis (days - weeks)
- Full recovery
- Root cause analysis
- Plan updates
- Team debrief
- Regulatory reporting

4.4 CRISIS COMMUNICATION PLAN
-------------------------------

Internal Communications:
- Initial Alert: Email + SMS to all staff (within 15 min)
- Status Updates: Hourly during crisis
- All-Hands Call: Within 2 hours of crisis
- Resolution Notice: When resolved
- Post-Mortem: Within 48 hours

Client Communications:
- Initial Notice: If trading impacted (within 1 hour)
- Status Updates: Every 4 hours during outage
- Resolution Notice: When restored
- Detailed Report: Within 5 business days

Regulatory Communications:
- Immediate: For market-impacting events
- Next Business Day: For significant operational issues
- Within 5 Days: Detailed written report
- Follow-up: As required

Media/Public:
- Holding Statement: Within 2 hours if public
- Detailed Statement: Within 24 hours
- FAQ: As needed
- Media Briefing: For major events

Communication Templates:
- Email templates pre-drafted

☐ No household access to work systems

Testing:

- Annual full WFH day

------------------------------

Authorization:
- Only designated traders approved
- Dual approval required (Trader + Risk)
- Additional monitoring during remote trading
- Enhanced audit logging

Technical Setup:
- Dedicated secure connection
- Hardware 2FA required
- No public Wi-Fi allowed
- Screen recording enabled
- Restricted trading hours
- Lower position limits

Risk Controls:
- Reduced risk limits (50% of normal)
- More frequent risk checks
- Real-time P&L monitoring
- Enhanced position monitoring
- Immediate escalation thresholds

7. FINANCIAL IMPACT & INSURANCE
================================================================================

7.1 COST ANALYSIS
------------------

Fixed Costs (Annual):
- Primary DC: $2M
- DR DC: $1.5M
- Backup DC: $500K
- Network: $1M
- Cloud Services: $500K
- Staff: $5M
- Insurance: $200K
Total: $10.7M/year

Variable Costs (per incident):
- Failover execution: $10K-50K
- Extended DR operations: $50K-100K/day
- Data recovery: $10K-100K
- Reputation impact: Varies

Revenue Impact (per hour of downtime):
- Lost trading revenue: $200K-500K
- Client compensation: $50K-100K
- Regulatory fines: $0-1M+
- Reputational damage: Unquantifiable

ROI Calculation:
Without DR: 1 major incident = $10M+ loss
With DR: Prevents or minimizes loss
Breakeven: Prevent one 24-hour outage every 5 years

7.2 INSURANCE COVERAGE
-----------------------

Cyber Insurance:
- Coverage: $10M
- Deductible: $100K
- Scope: Data breach, ransomware, system failure
- Exclusions: War, nuclear, acts of government

Business Interruption:
- Coverage: $5M
- Waiting Period: 8 hours
- Coverage Period: 30 days
- Includes: Lost revenue, extra expenses

Errors & Omissions:
- Coverage: $25M
- Deductible: $500K
- Scope: Trading errors, system failures causing client loss

Property & Equipment:
- Coverage: Replacement value
- Scope: Data center equipment, office

Directors & Officers:
- Coverage: $10M
- Scope: Management liability

Annual Premium: ~$200K total

Claims Process:
1. Notify insurer within 24 hours
2. Document all costs and impacts
3. Preserve evidence
4. Cooperate with investigation
5. Submit detailed claim within 30 days
